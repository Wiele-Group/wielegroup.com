<section className="mb-14">
  <h2 className="text-display-md text-white text-balance mb-5">
    §4 · Recommendation History
  </h2>
  <p className="text-body-md text-silver mb-4">
    Engines that have cited a source before are statistically more
    likely to cite it again. Recommendation History is the compounding
    loop and the reason early citation matters disproportionately —
    every accepted citation strengthens the next one.
  </p>
  <p className="text-body-md text-silver mb-4">
    §4 tracks two derived signals: the count of distinct prompts on
    which a brand was cited in the prior 90-day window (breadth), and
    the count of repeat citations on the same prompt across consecutive
    engine runs (depth). Depth without breadth indicates a single
    captured prompt; breadth without depth indicates volatile citation
    eligibility. Both rising together is the compounding signal.
  </p>
  <p className="text-body-md text-silver">
    <strong className="text-white">How Wiele scores it.</strong> Rolling
    90-day window across the prompt panel. Authority-tier engagements
    receive a quarterly Recommendation History narrative — which prompts
    became repeat-citation eligible, which slipped, and which compounded
    against the named competitor set. Pro receives biannually; Starter
    receives the breadth/depth pair but not the narrative.

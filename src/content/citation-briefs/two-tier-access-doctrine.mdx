  </p>

    <em> cognitive access</em> (cognitively elaborating its
    content). They were designing a web-based instruction system
    for architecture students. They were 25 years early to the
    problem that now decides which brand the AI engine cites.
  </p>

<section className="mb-14">
  <h2 className="text-display-md text-white text-balance mb-5">
    Where the doctrine comes from — Palermo, 1998.
  </h2>
  <p className="text-body-md text-silver mb-4">
    The philosophical origin sits in a short paper from the
    Italian National Research Council (Consiglio Nazionale delle
    Ricerche, CNR). R. Corrao and G. Fulantelli, at the
    Institute for Education and Training Technologies in Palermo,
    were building a web-based instruction system for University
    of Palermo architecture students. Their paper —
    <em> Cognitive accessibility to information on the Web</em> —
    drew a careful distinction the wider web-design field never
    fully internalised.
  </p>
  <p className="text-body-md text-silver mb-4">
    Reaching a page, they argued, is not the same as
    cognitively elaborating its content. A learner can request a
    resource, render it, scroll it, and walk away without
    having learned anything. The HTTP transaction succeeded; the
    cognitive transaction did not. They called the first
    <em> information access</em> and the second
    <em> cognitive access</em>, and they argued that web design
    that ignored the second tier was design that worked only on
    paper.
  </p>
  <p className="text-body-md text-silver mb-4">
    Their answer was a set of on-page scaffolds they called the
    <em> Working Tools Bar</em> — post-its, markers, page-marks,
    a kit-bag for saved fragments, an operation-aware history
    they named <em>Iter</em>. The tools were architectural; what
    matters today is the doctrine underneath them. Corrao and
    Fulantelli named the wedge before LLMs existed, before the
    iPhone existed, before mobile-first design existed. The
    architecture of the problem outlasted their architecture of
    the solution.
  </p>
  <p className="text-body-md text-silver">
    The AEO era brings the wedge back, harder. AI answer engines
    do not retrieve passages — they cognitively elaborate them
    into answers. A page that does not present an extractable,
    self-contained answer fails AI cognitive access for exactly
    the reason Corrao identified in 1998. The doctrine is not
    new. The cost of ignoring it is.
  </p>
</section>

<section className="mb-14">
  <h2 className="text-display-md text-white text-balance mb-5">
    Why pages that load still fail.
  </h2>
  <p className="text-body-md text-silver mb-6">
    Four recurring failure modes turn a fast, indexed page into
    one the engine cannot extract from. Each is invisible to
    Lighthouse. Each is fixable inside a sprint.
  </p>
  <Reveal stagger={0.06} className="grid gap-4">
    <div className="rounded-[var(--radius-lg)] border border-[var(--color-border-default)] bg-[var(--color-surface-elevated)] p-6 md:p-7">
      <div className="flex items-baseline gap-4 mb-3">
        <p className="text-body-xs font-mono text-electric">01</p>
        <h3 className="text-heading-md text-white">Anaphora across paragraph boundaries</h3>
      </div>
      <p className="text-body-md text-silver">
        Pronouns and demonstratives — <em>this</em>, <em>that</em>,
        <em> it</em>, <em>they</em> — that refer back across a
        paragraph break lose their referent the moment the engine
        chunks the passage. The human reader inferred the
        antecedent; the extractor sees a passage with a dangling
        pronoun and skips it. Each H2 block must be its own
        closed referent system.
      </p>
    </div>
    <div className="rounded-[var(--radius-lg)] border border-[var(--color-border-default)] bg-[var(--color-surface-elevated)] p-6 md:p-7">
      <div className="flex items-baseline gap-4 mb-3">
        <p className="text-body-xs font-mono text-electric">02</p>
        <h3 className="text-heading-md text-white">Heading hierarchy that does not segment the answer</h3>
      </div>
      <p className="text-body-md text-silver">
        Marketing-style headings (<em>The Future of Search</em>,
        <em> Why It Matters</em>) read well to humans and tell the
        extractor nothing. A heading should name the answer it
        introduces. Declarative headings outperform rhetorical
        headings across every engine class because the heading
        tree IS the engine&apos;s passage-retrieval index.
      </p>
    </div>
    <div className="rounded-[var(--radius-lg)] border border-[var(--color-border-default)] bg-[var(--color-surface-elevated)] p-6 md:p-7">
      <div className="flex items-baseline gap-4 mb-3">
        <p className="text-body-xs font-mono text-electric">03</p>
        <h3 className="text-heading-md text-white">Visual emphasis without semantic weight</h3>
      </div>
      <p className="text-body-md text-silver">
        A phrase styled large and bold in the design system but
        wrapped in a plain <code>&lt;span&gt;</code> reads as
        important to the eye and as body prose to the parser.
        Importance has to be expressed in HTML semantics
        (<code>&lt;strong&gt;</code>, <code>&lt;h2&gt;</code>,
        <code> &lt;dt&gt;</code>) before any CSS treatment lands
        on top. Pretty markup with semantic body text is the
        commonest agency-built failure pattern.
      </p>
    </div>
    <div className="rounded-[var(--radius-lg)] border border-[var(--color-border-default)] bg-[var(--color-surface-elevated)] p-6 md:p-7">
      <div className="flex items-baseline gap-4 mb-3">
        <p className="text-body-xs font-mono text-electric">04</p>
        <h3 className="text-heading-md text-white">Single-source self-reference</h3>
      </div>
      <p className="text-body-md text-silver">
        The page asserts facts and cites nothing. No Schema.org
        markup, no named author, no inbound entity links, no
        sourced statistic, no dated revision. The engine has no
        signal to weigh the claim and no fragment of citation
        graph to attach the page to. Authority is something the
        page either participates in or sits outside of. Self-
        reference alone keeps the page outside.
      </p>
    </div>
  </Reveal>
</section>

<section className="mb-14">
  <h2 className="text-display-md text-white text-balance mb-5">
    The three tests Wiele uses.
  </h2>
  <p className="text-body-md text-silver mb-4">
    Cognitive access is not a vibe. It is auditable against three
    named tests from the cognitive-psychology and usability
    canon. Wiele applies all three on every page that needs to
    be cited.
  </p>
  <ol className="space-y-4 text-body-md text-silver list-decimal pl-6">
    <li>
      <strong className="text-white">Krug&apos;s Trunk Test —
      5-second self-evidence.</strong> Steve Krug&apos;s 2014
      diagnostic: imagine being dumped on the page with no
      context. Within five seconds, can a reader identify what
      the page is, what it answers, where it sits in the site,
      and what to do next? If the page fails that test for a
      human reader, it fails it for an extractor running on a
      tighter time budget.
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong className="text-white">Whalen&apos;s Six Minds audit
      grid.</strong> John Whalen&apos;s 2019 framework: a customer
      experience is not one thing but six parallel cognitive
      processes — Vision and Attention, Wayfinding, Memory,
      Language, Decision-Making, Emotion. A page that lands on
      five of the six and leaks the sixth is a page that loads
      and fails. The grid converts cognitive access into a
      6-row audit any operator can execute.
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong className="text-white">Jones et al. 50-millisecond
      first-impression test.</strong> Jones, Fitzpatrick and
      Chassy (Liverpool Hope University, 2015): aesthetic
      judgement on a web page locks in at 50 milliseconds and
      persists. The three engineerable variables are
      prototypicality (does the page match what the reader
      already expects an authoritative page to look like?),
      moderate visual complexity (rich enough to signal
      authority, not so dense it overflows working memory), and
      processing fluency (clean typography, predictable layout,
      no cognitive friction in the first viewport).
    </li>
  </ol>
</section>

<section className="mb-14">
  <h2 className="text-display-md text-white text-balance mb-5">
    What cognitive access requires — the seven signals.
  </h2>
  <p className="text-body-md text-silver mb-6">
    Seven engineering decisions, applied together, move a page
    from information-accessible to cognitively-accessible. Each
    is auditable. Each is shippable inside a two-to-four-week
    sprint.
  </p>
  <Reveal stagger={0.05} className="grid gap-3">
    <div className="rounded-[var(--radius-lg)] border border-[var(--color-border-default)] bg-[var(--color-surface-elevated)] p-5 md:p-6">
      <p className="text-body-md text-white mb-1"><strong>01 · Entity-rich first paragraph.</strong></p>
      <p className="text-body-md text-silver">The opener names the entities the page is about — brand, framework, category, named author — in the first 50 to 100 words. Entity prominence on first appearance lifts both human salience and crawler resolution.</p>
    </div>
    <div className="rounded-[var(--radius-lg)] border border-[var(--color-border-default)] bg-[var(--color-surface-elevated)] p-5 md:p-6">
      <p className="text-body-md text-white mb-1"><strong>02 · Self-contained H2 answer blocks.</strong></p>
      <p className="text-body-md text-silver">Every H2 stands alone. A reader landing on the section via anchor link, or an extractor pulling that block in isolation, gets a complete answer without needing the sections above or below.</p>
    </div>
    <div className="rounded-[var(--radius-lg)] border border-[var(--color-border-default)] bg-[var(--color-surface-elevated)] p-5 md:p-6">
      <p className="text-body-md text-white mb-1"><strong>03 · Working-memory-safe chunking.</strong></p>
      <p className="text-body-md text-silver">Cowan&apos;s 4-chunk cap on working memory: visible vertical lists, callouts, and option sets stay at four or fewer items. Longer enumerations get sub-grouped. Compression beats truncation.</p>
    </div>
    <div className="rounded-[var(--radius-lg)] border border-[var(--color-border-default)] bg-[var(--color-surface-elevated)] p-5 md:p-6">
      <p className="text-body-md text-white mb-1"><strong>04 · JSON-LD structured data, inlined.</strong></p>
      <p className="text-body-md text-silver">Article, FAQPage, HowTo, Person, BreadcrumbList — server-rendered into the HTML response, not injected after hydration. Non-JS crawlers see the schema; non-JS extractors use it.</p>
    </div>
    <div className="rounded-[var(--radius-lg)] border border-[var(--color-border-default)] bg-[var(--color-surface-elevated)] p-5 md:p-6">
      <p className="text-body-md text-white mb-1"><strong>05 · Internal entity links, not topical anchors.</strong></p>
      <p className="text-body-md text-silver">Anchor text is the entity name (<em>Citation Score™</em>) not a generic phrase (<em>learn more</em>). Internal links form an entity graph the engine can traverse; topical anchors form a dead-letter office.</p>
    </div>
    <div className="rounded-[var(--radius-lg)] border border-[var(--color-border-default)] bg-[var(--color-surface-elevated)] p-5 md:p-6">
      <p className="text-body-md text-white mb-1"><strong>06 · Explicit re-elaboration of the question inside the answer.</strong></p>
      <p className="text-body-md text-silver">A passage that answers <em>how do agencies get cited in AI answers</em> repeats the question&apos;s entities and verbs inside the answer body, not just in the heading. Re-elaboration is what makes the passage extractable on its own.</p>
    </div>
    <div className="rounded-[var(--radius-lg)] border border-[var(--color-border-default)] bg-[var(--color-surface-elevated)] p-5 md:p-6">
      <p className="text-body-md text-white mb-1"><strong>07 · Versioned date stamps and authority signals.</strong></p>
      <p className="text-body-md text-silver">Visible <code>datePublished</code> and <code>dateModified</code>, named author, named methodology source, dated revision history. The engine&apos;s recency filter and authority weighting both reward the visible trail.</p>
    </div>
  </Reveal>
</section>
